# COMPREHENSIVE RESEARCH REPORT: LOTUS BAKERIES

## Executive Summary

This report provides an in-depth analysis of Lotus Bakeries, a multinational snack food company with a strong global presence. Founded in 1932, the company has demonstrated consistent revenue growth, with a remarkable 16% increase in 2024, reaching €1,232 million in revenue. The Lotus Biscoff brand remains the primary driver, accounting for 56% of the group's branded revenue. The company is strategically positioned for future expansion, with a focus on internationalization, product diversification, and sustainability. Despite facing challenges such as production capacity constraints and increasing competition, Lotus Bakeries is well-equipped to navigate these challenges through innovation and strategic partnerships.

## 1. Current Company Status and Health

Lotus Bakeries has reported strong financial performance in 2024, with revenue growth of 16% year-over-year, reaching €1,232 million. The company's net profit exceeded €156 million, with operating income surging 18.2%. The Lotus Biscoff brand continues to drive growth, with a 20% increase in revenue in 2024, adding €100 million of incremental sales.

### Financial Highlights

* Revenue: €1,232 million (16% growth year-over-year)
* Net profit: €156 million
* Operating income: 18.2% increase
* Lotus Biscoff revenue: 56% of group's branded revenue (20% growth in 2024)

### Operational Status

The company's production lines are running at full capacity, with a strong international expansion strategy paying off. The three strategic pillars of the company are performing strongly, with Lotus Biscoff being the primary driver. The company is driving organic expansion rather than relying on price increases.

### Market Position

Lotus Bakeries is listed on the Brussels Stock Exchange (ticker: LOTB.BR) and is a family-controlled business with the Boone & Stevens family as major shareholders. The company has a strong global presence as a multinational snack food company.

## 2. Historical Company Performance

Lotus Bakeries was founded in 1932 by three brothers, Jan, Emiel, and Henri Boone, in Lembeke, Belgium. The company has delivered impressive 10% annual revenue growth for 35 consecutive years. The company went public in 1988 on the Brussels Stock Exchange and has consistently increased gross profit, EBITDA, and net income.

### Key Milestones

* 1932: Company founding and creation of original Lotus biscuit
* 1988: Initial public offering
* Recent years: Achieved over €1 billion in annual revenue milestone
* 2024: Record financial performance with €1.23 billion revenue

### Challenges

### Opportunities
